About NASCP

Vision, Mission & Mandate

The National AIDS, Viral Hepatitis and STIs Control Programme (NASCP) leads and coordinates Nigeria’s health-sector response to HIV, viral hepatitis and STIs.

Vision

An AIDS-free Nigeria with zero new infections, zero stigma & discrimination, and zero AIDS-related deaths.

Mission

To reduce morbidity and mortality from HIV, viral hepatitis and STIs by coordinating, guiding and strengthening the national health-sector response across all levels of care.

Mandate

Provide national leadership, policies, standards and coordination for prevention, treatment, care & support, and strategic information.

See full mandate

Our Mandate in Detail

  • Policy & Guidelines: develop, publish and update national policies, guidelines and SOPs.
  • Prevention: drive evidence-based prevention including testing, PMTCT, key & priority populations, and risk-reduction.
  • Treatment, Care & Support: expand access to quality ART, differentiated care, adherence & retention, and psychosocial support.
  • Laboratory Services: standards, quality systems and networks for VL/EID/recency and related diagnostics.
  • Supply Chain: ensure availability of commodities through quantification, procurement & logistics coordination.
  • Strategic Information: surveillance, M&E, data systems, analytics and performance management.
  • Programme Stewardship: planning, financing, partnerships and multi-level coordination with states & partners.
  • Advocacy & Community: stigma reduction, human rights, ACSM and meaningful community engagement.
