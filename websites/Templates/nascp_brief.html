About NASCP

Programme Brief

Health-sector leadership for Nigeria’s response to HIV, viral hepatitis and STIs.

The National AIDS, Viral Hepatitis and STIs Control Programme (NASCP) is the health-sector lead for Nigeria’s response to HIV, viral hepatitis and sexually transmitted infections. Situated within the Department of Public Health, Federal Ministry of Health, NASCP provides national stewardship—setting policies and guidelines, coordinating partners and states, and ensuring quality, equitable services across the continuum of prevention, testing, treatment, care and support.

NASCP’s work spans programme design and scale-up; workforce and facility readiness; laboratory systems—including viral load, early infant diagnosis and related quality assurance; and commodity security through quantification and supply-chain coordination. The Programme also leads strategic information—surveillance, monitoring and evaluation, data systems and analytics—to drive evidence-based planning and performance improvement.

Through multi-level collaboration with state ministries of health, agencies, civil society, communities and development partners, NASCP advances stigma-free, rights-respecting services, prioritizes key and priority populations, and strengthens integration with primary health care. The goal is clear: reduce new infections and deaths, improve health outcomes and accelerate progress toward epidemic control—while building a resilient, data-driven health system that leaves no one behind.
